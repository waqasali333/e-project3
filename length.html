Length Converter


Length units have ancient origins, with civilizations like the Egyptians using the cubit. The Romans introduced the foot and the inch, influencing medieval Europe's systems. The metric system, with the meter as its base, emerged during the French Revolution. In 1983, the meter was redefined based on the speed of light. The International System of Units (SI) now serves as the global standard. Conversion tools evolved from manual charts to digital calculators. Online tools further simplified length conversions, reflecting a continuous human quest for standardized and efficient measurement systems.
